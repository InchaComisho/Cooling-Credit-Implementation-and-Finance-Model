# How to Read Weak Results

Conceptual Cooling Credit simulations do not require every project to pay back quickly. A weak result is decision information, not something to hide.

## 1. Weak results are not failure

They may reflect high CAPEX; incomplete avoided-loss valuation; low medical, disaster, or insurance assumptions; omitted peak-power and public-space value; omitted food-security or water value; a short horizon; low credit prices; or no public and insurer payment.

## 2. What weak results reveal

They identify projects that private capital cannot fund alone, cases needing public support, indicators MRV should add, benefits not yet valued, and assumptions controlling investment decisions.

## 3. Why implementation may still be necessary

The base case is not the only relevant future. If temperature, heavy rain, medical cost, cooling demand, insurance claims, and agricultural loss rise, no-action losses compound. Cooling Credits should therefore be appraised as prepaid risk reduction, not simply as an investment expected to make a quick profit.

Scenario values are not predictions. Pilot evidence should determine whether to stop, redesign, support, or scale.
