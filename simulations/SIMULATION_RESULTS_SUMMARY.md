# Cooling Credit Simulation Results Summary

Default assumptions produce modest and mixed results. The purpose is not to guarantee outcomes, but to show why local MRV data, pilot projects, and explicit no-action costs are necessary.

| Simulation | Default Payback | Main Finding | Interpretation |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Urban Cooling Cost-Benefit | Year 11 | Payback appears after medium-term avoided costs accumulate | Strong candidate for municipal pilot projects |
| Disaster Risk Avoidance | Year 12 | Benefits depend on avoided flood-loss assumptions | Useful for insurers, municipalities, and watershed planning |
| Soil Recovery Agriculture | No payback under default assumptions | Direct farm cash flow alone is insufficient | Requires food-security, water-risk, public-support, or supply-chain valuation |
| Cooling Credit Finance | Year 9 | Portfolio approach improves viability | Strongest case for SPV / fund design |

The simulations do not show that every Cooling Credit project is automatically profitable. They show where the model is strongest, where public support is needed, and why local MRV data is essential before scaling.

See [How to Read Weak Results](HOW_TO_READ_WEAK_RESULTS.md).
