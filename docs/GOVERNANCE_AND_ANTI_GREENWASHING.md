# Cooling Credit Governance and Anti-Greenwashing Model

This document defines governance principles for preventing Cooling Credits from becoming vague ESG labels, speculative certificates, or emission-offset excuses.

Cooling Credits must remain connected to measured physical cooling outcomes and restoration of natural cooling functions.

---

## 1. Core Governance Principle

```text
No measured cooling, no Cooling Credit.
```

A Cooling Credit must not be issued merely because a project is green, popular, visually attractive, or climate-related.

It should be issued only when the project produces verified cooling or verified restoration of natural cooling functions.

---

## 2. Governance Objectives

The governance system should protect:

1. the meaning of the term Cooling Credit;
2. the credibility of MRV;
3. the separation from carbon-offset claims;
4. the rights and benefits of local communities;
5. the integrity of financial flows;
6. the ecological safety of projects;
7. the transparency of public claims;
8. the long-term maintenance of cooling functions.

---

## 3. Main Greenwashing Risks

| Risk | Description | Control |
|---|---|---|
| Label inflation | Calling any green project a Cooling Credit | Require boundary check and MRV |
| Paper cooling | Issuing credits without physical cooling data | Require measured indicators |
| Offset misuse | Using Cooling Credits to justify emissions | Prohibit automatic offset claims |
| Over-crediting | Issuing too many credits for weak or temporary effects | Apply uncertainty and duration discounts |
| Double counting | Multiple parties claiming the same outcome | Use registry and claim tracking |
| Community extraction | External buyers capture value without local benefit | Require local benefit disclosure |
| Ecological harm | Cooling project damages ecosystems | Require ecological risk review |
| Speculation | Certificates trade without maintaining cooling systems | Link validity to maintenance and MRV |

---

## 4. Required Claim Language

Cooling Credit claims should be precise.

Preferred wording:

```text
This project produced verified cooling contribution under the stated baseline, boundary, monitoring period, and MRV method.
```

Avoid:

```text
This project offsets our emissions.
This project makes us carbon neutral.
This project solves climate change.
This project guarantees permanent cooling.
```

Unless separately certified under another system, Cooling Credits should not be presented as carbon offsets.

---

## 5. Minimum Disclosure Requirements

Each issued Cooling Credit or outcome claim should disclose:

1. project location;
2. project operator;
3. buyer or payer where applicable;
4. intervention type;
5. baseline condition;
6. measurement boundary;
7. monitoring period;
8. indicators measured;
9. uncertainty range;
10. risk and side-effect assessment;
11. third-party verification status;
12. validity period;
13. whether the claim is cooling contribution, not emissions offset.

---

## 6. Registry and Claim Tracking

A registry should record:

- project ID;
- credit issuance date;
- monitoring period;
- buyer or claimant;
- quantity or score;
- method used;
- expiration date;
- retirement status;
- public claim restrictions;
- links to MRV reports.

This prevents double counting and makes public claims traceable.

---

## 7. Project Governance Roles

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Project operator | Implements and maintains the cooling project |
| MRV provider | Measures and reports outcomes |
| Verifier | Checks data, baseline, and methodology |
| Registry administrator | Tracks issuance, claims, retirement, and validity |
| Local authority | Ensures public interest and local compatibility |
| Community representative | Confirms local benefit and concerns |
| Buyer / payer | Funds verified outcomes and uses claims responsibly |
| Governance board | Reviews disputes, risks, and methodology updates |

---

## 8. Suspension Conditions

Credit issuance should pause or stop if:

- MRV data is missing;
- sensors fail without replacement;
- maintenance stops;
- cooling effects cannot be attributed;
- ecological harm is observed;
- water stress worsens;
- claims are misleading;
- double counting occurs;
- the project operator violates reporting obligations.

---

## 9. Local Community Protection

Cooling projects often operate in public spaces, farmland, forests, watersheds, coasts, or shared ecosystems.

Governance should require:

- local explanation;
- transparent benefit sharing;
- community complaint channels;
- avoidance of land or water grabbing;
- respect for existing livelihoods;
- monitoring of unintended burdens;
- public access to summarized results.

Cooling Credits should not extract value from local ecosystems while leaving maintenance burdens to local communities.

---

## 10. Financial Integrity

Financial governance should require:

- separation of project funds and operating funds;
- clear revenue allocation;
- maintenance reserve;
- MRV budget;
- audit trail;
- conflict-of-interest disclosure;
- restrictions on speculative claims;
- compliance with local financial and securities laws.

Cooling Credits should begin as outcome-based finance and verified contribution accounting, not as unregulated speculative products.

## 12. Summary

Cooling Credits can become credible only if they are protected from misuse.

The rule is simple:

```text
Cooling claim = measured cooling + transparent MRV + responsible governance.
```

Without these, a Cooling Credit risks becoming another paper certificate detached from the Earth system it is supposed to cool.
