# Cooling Credit Investor and SPV Model

## Financing architecture

```text
Investors
  ↓
Cooling Project SPV / Fund
  ↓
Cooling projects
  ↓
Measured outcomes
  ↓
Outcome payments + credit revenue + avoided-cost contracts
  ↓
Investor return + community return + reinvestment
```

The SPV ring-fences contracts, assets, data rights, reserves, and liabilities. It should not convert uncertain social benefit into guaranteed yield.

## Sources, uses, and return metrics

Uses include CAPEX, annual OPEX, MRV, registry, insurance, development, replacement, and reserves. Revenue may combine municipal outcome payments, insurer risk-reduction payments, utility peak-reduction contracts, real-estate value capture, agriculture/tourism benefit sharing, and conservatively issued Cooling Credits.

Calculate payback from cumulative investor cash flow; ROI as undiscounted lifetime net investor gain divided by initial invested capital; NPV using the investor hurdle/discount rate; and IRR only where cash-flow sign changes produce an economically meaningful root. Report gross project benefits separately from contracted cash revenue.

## Sensitivity and diligence

Test credit price/volume, construction overrun, commissioning delay, asset survival, OPEX, verification failure, outcome-payment haircut, discount rate, and contract renewal. Publish base, downside, and severe downside cases. Investors should review additionality, permanence, ownership, claims, local consent, procurement, counterparty credit, data quality, tax, securities, and insolvency treatment.

## Why speculative trading comes later

Physical performance, MRV, registry controls, buyer claims, and retirement must precede secondary-market liquidity. Early speculation can detach price from outcomes, invite double counting, and transfer volatility to communities. The preferred first revenues are contracted payments from parties that receive measurable avoided cost; credits supplement rather than substitute for those contracts.

## Distribution policy

Apply revenues first to safe operations, MRV, registry and debt obligations; then fund reserves and a disclosed community return; distribute residual cash to investors; and reinvest an agreed share. No return is guaranteed. Use independent verification and annual audited cash-flow reconciliation.
