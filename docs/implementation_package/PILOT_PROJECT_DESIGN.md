# Urban Cooling Pilot Project Design

## Recommended first pilot

Select a school, hospital, elderly-care facility, or station plaza with high exposure, public ownership or clear consent, reliable meters, and a comparable control area. Define a walkable intervention zone and protect access for vulnerable users.

## Measurement and intervention

Collect one warm-season baseline where possible, or at minimum 8–12 weeks spanning representative hot days. Record WBGT, air and surface temperature, humidity, footfall, electricity, heat incidents, rainfall, runoff/infiltration, water use, and vegetation condition.

Implement a site-specific bundle: shade trees; shade structures; rainwater capture; water-retentive pavement; rooftop/wall greening; mist cooling using rainwater or recycled water; and organic matter/humus circulation for soil beds. Do not use potable-water misting where water stress or hygiene controls make it inappropriate.

## MRV, stakeholders, and costs

Track paired differences in WBGT, surface temperature, peak cooling kWh/kW, heatstroke or first-aid incidence, infiltration/storage, green coverage, plant survival, water quality, and maintenance uptime. Stakeholders include site users, municipality, facility owner, health and water authorities, utility, contractor, verifier, insurer, funder, and community representatives.

Cost categories include design, permits, civil works, vegetation, irrigation/storage, sensors, data, MRV, verification, operations, replacement, engagement, contingency, and decommissioning. Benefits include avoided medical and energy cost, reduced flood burden, comfort/access, asset protection, water retention, biodiversity, and local spending.

## Risk controls and decision gates

Control heat/legionella and slip hazards, structural loading, tree survival, drought supply, sensor drift, cyber/privacy risk, displacement, double counting, and benefit overstatement. Pre-register formulas; use conservative attribution; report confidence ranges; maintain an incident log; and reserve funds for maintenance. Proceed only if safety, data completeness, additionality, and conservative net-benefit thresholds are met.
