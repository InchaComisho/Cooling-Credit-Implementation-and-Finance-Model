# Municipal Cooling Credit Implementation Roadmap

## Objective and outcome logic

Municipalities can redirect part of future heat, health, energy, and disaster burdens into preventive assets. The causal chain is: shaded and water-sensitive places → lower heat exposure and runoff → lower medical cost, cooling demand, and disaster burden → verified outcomes eligible for outcome finance.

## Priority sites and baseline

Start with schools, hospitals, elderly-care facilities, station plazas, parks, and dense residential areas. Establish at least one warm-season baseline using calibrated sensors, utility bills, anonymized heatstroke records, infiltration tests, and mapped green coverage. Define control sites and data governance before construction.

## Seven implementation phases

| Phase | Action | Decision gate |
|---|---|---|
| 0: Desk design and baseline preparation | Map heat, vulnerability, runoff, ownership, and costs | Comparable baseline and intervention boundary approved |
| 1: Small pilot project | Install a bounded bundle at one priority site | Safety, procurement, and maintenance responsibilities confirmed |
| 2: MRV and verification | Measure WBGT, surface temperature, electricity, heatstroke, infiltration, and green coverage | Independent verifier accepts methods and uncertainty |
| 3: Trial Cooling Credit issuance | Issue a conservative quantity tied to additional verified outcomes | No double counting; serial numbers and claims policy active |
| 4: Outcome-based finance and public/private funding | Contract payments from beneficiaries | Payment triggers and downside allocation documented |
| 5: Registry, retirement, and institutionalization | Register, transfer, retire, audit, and report units | Municipal ordinance or program rule adopted |
| 6: Sector expansion | Extend to agriculture, forests, coasts, tourism, and drylands | Local evidence supports transferability |

## Indicators and responsibilities

| Indicator | Method | Typical owner |
|---|---|---|
| WBGT and surface temperature | Fixed/mobile sensors; paired control | Public-health or environment office |
| Cooling electricity demand | Weather-normalized meter data | Facility manager and utility |
| Heatstroke cases | Privacy-preserving incidence/rate | Health authority |
| Rainwater infiltration | Field tests and storage logs | Water/drainage office |
| Green coverage | Geospatial audit and survival checks | Parks office |

## Funding and governance

Blend municipal budgets and national subsidies with insurers, utilities, real-estate developers, and ESG investors. Public money should fund public-good baselines and access; private/outcome capital can fund scalable assets. Publish assumptions, procurement, conflicts, reversals, maintenance, grievance routes, and credit retirement. Scale only when verified benefits remain material under conservative sensitivity cases.
