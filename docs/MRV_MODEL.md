## Overview

MRV is the core of Cooling Credit credibility.

MRV means:

- Measurement;
- Reporting;
- Verification.

Cooling Credits should evaluate not only CO₂-related indicators but also heat, water, soil, forests, oceans, ecosystems, medical burdens, energy demand, disaster loss, and economic loss.

---

## 1. Purpose of MRV

MRV exists to:

- confirm whether measurable cooling outcomes occurred;
- prevent overstatement;
- provide explainable data to funders;
- support public subsidies and outcome-based payments;
- support decisions by investors, insurers, municipalities, and companies;
- prevent double counting and unjustified issuance.

## 3. Baseline Setting

Baseline design may use before-after comparison, nearby non-intervention zones, multi-year averages, weather and seasonal correction, remote sensing, official statistics, and sensor-based continuous monitoring.

Baselines should be transparent and conservative because they strongly affect credit issuance.

---

## 4. Measurement Methods

Useful data sources include local sensors, weather data, WBGT meters, thermal cameras, satellite and aerial imagery, soil-moisture sensors, water-quality sensors, electricity-use data, medical statistics, agricultural yield data, insurance and disaster-loss data, and resident or user surveys.

Multiple data sources should be compared to reduce error.

---

## 5. Reporting

Reports should include project overview, target area, interventions, monitoring period, baseline, measurement methods, results, correction methods, uncertainty, side effects, credit issuance volume, and verification results.

---

## 6. Verification

Verification should check data integrity, measurement validity, baseline conservatism, weather correction, side-effect disclosure, double-counting prevention, and conservative issuance.

Third-party verification is essential for trust.

---

## 7. Composite Score

A single indicator can distort project design. A composite model is preferable:

```text
Cooling Credit Score
= Physical Cooling Effect
+ Recovery of Natural Cooling Functions
+ Water-Cycle Recovery
+ Disaster-Risk Reduction
+ Reduction in Socio-Economic Losses
- Side Effects / Ecological Risks
```

Weighting should differ by region and project type.

---

## 8. Uncertainty

Climate, water cycles, and ecosystems contain uncertainty. Issuance should therefore be conservative, disclose uncertainty, rely on long-term monitoring, adjust when side effects appear, and emphasize observed results over speculative forecasts.
