## Overview

Public investment in Cooling Credits is justified when preventive cooling costs less than later medical care, emergency response, compensation, infrastructure repair, and recovery.

## 1. Rationale for Public Funding

Heat illness, floods, drought, crop losses, power stress, and water scarcity create public liabilities. Cooling projects can be treated as advance investment to reduce those liabilities, while benefits that cannot be fully captured by one private buyer remain publicly supported.

## 2. Disaster-Prevention Finance

Eligible risks include extreme heat, urban heat islands, drought, flooding, intense rainfall, storms, crop loss, water shortage, outages, marine heatwaves, and coastal economic damage. Restoring cooling and water circulation can complement—not replace—emergency preparedness and emissions reduction.

## 3. Public Funding Sources

- Environmental, adaptation, and disaster-prevention budgets
- Urban, water, agriculture, forestry, health, welfare, and school budgets
- Regional revitalization funds
- International climate funds and development-bank finance

## 4. Outcome-Based Subsidies

Funding can be staged from project approval to MRV-confirmed outcomes. Indicators may include lower WBGT or surface temperature, reduced cooling demand or heat illness, increased infiltration or soil moisture, stabilized yields, and lower flood or recovery losses.

## 5. Medical-Cost Reduction

Shade, vegetation, water-sensitive design, misting, and cooling around schools, hospitals, and elder-care facilities may reduce heat exposure. Any medical savings claim must use health data, an appropriate comparison, and conservative attribution.

## 6. Recovery-Cost Reduction

Infiltration, soil water retention, forest restoration, watershed management, and reduced urban drainage loads may lower some flood and drought damage. Funding decisions should compare lifecycle cost, maintenance, residual risk, and avoided-loss uncertainty.

## 7. International Support

Cooling Credit programs can integrate adaptation, health, food security, water, and disaster-risk objectives in arid areas, heatwave regions, vulnerable farming zones, coastal communities, and dense low-income urban districts.

## 8. Transparency Requirements

Public funding requires a prior plan, baseline, outcome indicators, MRV, independent verification, community explanation, safeguard monitoring, open data, procurement compliance, and a long-term maintenance plan.

## 9. Conclusion

Cooling Credits are not merely subsidies. They can structure prepaid, evidence-based risk-reduction investment that protects communities and reduces future fiscal burdens.
